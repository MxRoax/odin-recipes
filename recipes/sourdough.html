Sourdough Bread

Just about everything is better homemade, especially bread! While it may take a pinch more time and a handful of elbow grease, by the end you'll be enjoying more than just the fruits of your labor.

If cooking is an art, then baking is a science.

Equipment

  • Scale, not necessarity but extremely recommended
  • Dutch oven
  • Parchment paper

Ingredients

  • 60 mg sourdough starter (about 1/4)
  • 340 grams water (about 1 1/3 cup plus 2 table spoons)
  • 10 grams salt (about 1 1/2 teaspoons)
  • 500 grams bread flour (about 3 2/3 cups)

Steps

  1. In this first step we are assuming you have a ripe, active, and bubbly sourdough starter. With that starter you will mix all of your starter, water, bread flour, and salt into a large bowl and mix together until all the flour is encorporated and a shaggy dough is formed.

  2. This step will include a lot of waiting: Cover the dough and let it rest in a warm place (around 76°F) for 10-12 hours, in which it will both strengthen and double in size.

  3. After it has double in size, drop your dough onto a clean countertop and, with damp hands, form into a ball

  4. Cover and let rise for another 1-2 hours, until relaxed and puffed up.

  5. Place the dutch oven into the oven and then begin preheating both the oven and dutch oven to 500°F for 20 minutes.

    Now that your dutch oven is nice and toasty, add in your parchment paper and dough into the dutch oven. With the top on bake for 25 minutes on 450°F. Once those 25 minutes are up, continue baking for 20 more minutes with the top of the dutch oven off.

  6. Pull the bread out the oven and let cool before enjoying.
