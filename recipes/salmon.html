Pan-Seared Salmon

There’s nothing better to cook up as a middle-of-the-week, savory treat like a simple salmon filet. It’s hearty and packed with nutrients that pairs well with just about any carb and any vegetable. Similar to many fish filets, they can have an impeccably quick defrost time; meaning you can think of dinner at noon and have it neatly plated by evening.

In this recipe, we will be creating a basic panseared-salmon, though that just means that once you get a hang of the process you can experiment with whatever seasons or spices that you wish. If you›re feeling particularly fancy, you can make a glaze or sauce to top it all off.

Ingredients

  • Oil or Butter
  • Salt & Pepper, to taste
  • Fillet(s), whatever you think is a reasonable amount

Steps

  1. Season your salmon fillets with salt and pepper.

  2. Crank your stove up to around medium-high heat, and add just enough oil to coat the bottom of the pan. Wait until hot.

  3. Add salmon (there is much debate on which side goes first, so play around with whatever method you like) and leave it be for a few minutes.

  4. Once the salmon is ready you›ll be able to gently lift it from the pan without any resistance. If you›re having any trouble, let it sit for a little longer and try again.

  5. Cook the remaining side to your preference and serve.
