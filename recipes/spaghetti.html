Ingredients

  • Dried Spaghetti Noodles
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Italian Seasoning
  • Salt & Pepper, to Taste
  • Ground Meat
  • Parmesan Cheese

Steps

  1. Salt the water like the sea and until a rolling boil - this is the perfect time to be seasoning your meat - and then add your noodles. The package will tell you how long until al-dente, whether or not you follow it is none of my business.

  2. While that’s boiling, throw your hopefully seasoned meat onto the skillet and cook until mostly cooked — I don’t know how hot, people usually go medium hot — before draining.

  3. If you were wondering why we weren’t cooking all the way through, it’s because the remainder of the cooking will be done in the sauce. Poor that good sauce into that skillet and season it some more if you wish. I'd advise it to really encorporate the flavor.

  4. Once both the noodles and sauce are finished cooking, you can either merge them both into one pot or keep them separate. To stop the starch from sticking your noodles together, rinsing them with water (before putting the sauce onto them) will help with that.
